Diocesan Shrine and Parish of St. Clement

Angono's Church is dedicated to Saint Clement of Rome (also known as Pope Clement I). He was the fourth Pope after St. Peter, Linus, and Cletus, and served as the Catholic Pope from 92 to 99. He is regarded the first Apostolic Father of the Church and the first Apostolic Father of the Church (Anacletus). He can be identified in works of art by the anchor at his side. The Anchored Cross, also known as the Mariner's Cross, is also known as St Clement's Cross, after the manner in which he was killed. He was tossed into the water with a Mariner's Cross around his neck.

Binangonan

Ang Kalbaryo

The Cross on the Summit is significant not only in terms of religious importance, but also in terms of historical relevance. According to legend, a cross-shaped rock sprouted out of the peak, but lightning and an earthquake destroyed it. In 1873, the people replaced the ancient cross with a wooden cross on the same position. In honor of the Cross, a chapel was built on top of a hill overlooking Laguna. It is a popular destination during the holy week.

Location: Binangonan, Rizal

Facebook Page: https://www.facebook.com/kalbaryobinangonan/

Cardona

Diocesan Shrine and Parish of Our Lady of the Most Holy Rosary

Our Lady of the Holy Rosary parish in Cardona was created in 1872 by Franciscan missionaries who had been stationed in Morong since the 16th century and had erected mission sites along the shores of Laguna de Bay, which eventually grew into two parishes. When Cardona was still a sitio of Morong, there was claimed to be an image of Our Lady of the Holy Rosary preserved in the main church that would suddenly vanish at times. The statue would miraculously return to the church each time, and the church's keepers would invariably discover amorseco, a grass weed that grows abundantly in the Sapao area. They come to the conclusion that the Blessed Mother herself chose the location for her image to be placed.

Location: Cardona, Rizal

San Ildefonso de Toledo Parish

The Tanay Church, also known as Saint Ildefonsus of Toledo Parish Church (Spanish: Iglesia Parroquial de San Ildefonso de Toledo), is a Roman Catholic church in Tanay, Rizal Province, Philippines. The current church was started in 1773 and finished in 1783 after a ten-year construction period. The National Commission for Culture and the Arts designated it as a National Cultural Treasure Church in 2001. It is also one of the Diocese of Antipolo's seven Jubilee churches.

Taytay

St. John the Baptist Church

Saint John the Baptist Parish is in Taytay, Rizal's Barangay San Isidro. It is the Diocese of Antipolo's second oldest parish, having been founded in 1579. It was built in 1579 by Franciscan missionaries in Laguna Bay, originally from of light materials. Pedro Calungsod, a young child, was placed as an altar boy in the church by the Jesuits in 1666. During San Vitores' duty in Taytay, Calungsod would eventually become his assistant.

Teresa

Saint Rose of Lima

The parish of St. Rose of Lima is located in the Teresa Valley, historically known as Oroquieta, and was previously part of the Morong municipality before being annexed to Antipolo. The town's name was eventually changed to Sta from Oroquieta. Rosa as a tribute to the Patroness. For the third time in his life, Sta. Rosa was finally transferred to Teresa's authority in honor of Don Mariano Rodriquez Candelaria, a Spanish lawyer who fought for the town's independence. In 1903, a chapel in the camalig style was built to honor Sta Rosa.

Location: Teresa, Rizal
